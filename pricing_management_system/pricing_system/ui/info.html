Understanding Your Sales Dashboard

This guide explains every chart, KPI, table, and metric in the Sales Analytics dashboard. Each section explains how it works, what data it uses, what insight it provides, and — most importantly — what action you should take based on what you see. Use the search bar below to quickly find specific metrics, or filter by category.

01 Monthly Revenue Bar Chart ▼

Displays the total gross revenue generated each month over the selected date range. Each bar represents one month of sales.

⚙ How It Works
The chart aggregates transaction data by calendar month. Each bar represents the sum of all invoice amounts (gross revenue) for that month. Data is sourced from sales transactions after applying filters (portal, account, fulfillment mode, etc.). The taller the bar, the more revenue generated that month.
𝐀 Formula
Monthly Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) GROUP BY MONTH(transaction_date)
📊 Data Used
  • transaction_date — Date of the sale
  • invoice_amount — Gross amount of the invoice
  • portal — B2B / B2C (filtered)
💡 What to Look For
Growing trend = Business expanding. Sudden drop = Check for seasonality, stockouts, or competitive pressure. Flat plateau = Market saturation or need for new growth initiatives.
⚡ Actionable Insights
If revenue is declining: Investigate stock availability, pricing changes, and competitor activity. If growing: Identify what changed and replicate it across other channels.

02 Fulfillment Channel Revenue Doughnut Chart ▼

Shows the proportion of total revenue contributed by each fulfillment channel (FBA, FBM, Easy Ship, etc.).

⚙ How It Works
Revenue is grouped by the fulfillment mode. Each slice represents the percentage share of total revenue for that channel. The doughnut chart makes it easy to compare relative contributions at a glance.
𝐀 Formula
Channel Share = (SUM(revenue) per channel / SUM(revenue) all) × 100
📊 Data Used
  • fulfillment_channel — FBA / FBM / Easy Ship
  • invoice_amount — Revenue per transaction
💡 What to Look For
If one channel dominates (>70%), you have high concentration risk. If a channel with higher fees has growing share, check if the value justifies the cost.
⚡ Actionable Insights
Diversify across channels to reduce risk. If FBA is dominant, consider FBM for high-margin items. If a channel has low share but high refund rate, investigate whether to continue using it.

03 Top States by Orders Bar Chart ▼

Ranks states by the number of shipped orders placed from them. The top 10 states are shown.

⚙ How It Works
Orders are counted per billing/shipping state and sorted descending. Only the top 10 states appear to keep the chart readable.
𝐀 Formula
Orders by State = COUNT(order_id) GROUP BY billing_state
📊 Data Used
  • billing_state — Customer state
  • order_id — Unique order identifier
💡 What to Look For
High concentration in 1-2 states = Geographic risk. If those states face logistics issues, it impacts your entire business. Even distribution = Healthy geographic diversification.
⚡ Actionable Insights
Consider regional warehousing in top states to reduce delivery times. Target underpenetrated states with marketing campaigns. Evaluate carrier partnerships for high-volume states.

04 Monthly Revenue Trend Area Chart ▼

A filled line chart tracking gross revenue month-over-month across the entire period. The area fill emphasizes trend direction.

⚙ How It Works
Each point is the total gross revenue for a month. Points are connected by a smoothed line with a translucent fill below it, emphasizing both direction and magnitude of change.
𝐀 Formula
Monthly Rev = SUM(invoice_amount) GROUP BY DATE_TRUNC('month', transaction_date)
📊 Data Used
  • transaction_date — Date of transaction
  • invoice_amount — Gross invoice amount
💡 What to Look For
Upward slope = Healthy growth. Downward slope = Declining sales, investigate immediately. Sharp spikes/dips = One-time events (promotions, stockouts, holidays). Seasonal pattern = Recurring cycles to plan for.
⚡ Actionable Insights
Compare trend against same period last year for true growth. If trend is declining, check marketing ROI, competitor pricing, and inventory levels.

05 Month-over-Month Growth Bar Chart ▼

Percentage change in revenue from one month to the next. Green = growth, red = decline.

⚙ How It Works
For each month after the first, the percentage difference from the previous month is calculated. Green bars indicate positive growth; red bars indicate negative growth.
𝐀 Formula
MoM Growth % = ((Revenue(current) - Revenue(previous)) / Revenue(previous)) × 100
📊 Data Used
  • month — Month label
  • revenue — Aggregated monthly revenue
💡 What to Look For
Aim for consistent positive growth (green bars). Two or more consecutive red bars signal a worrying trend. 0% or near-zero = stagnation. Extreme swings (>±50%) = unusual one-time events.
⚡ Actionable Insights
After a decline month: Check stockouts, pricing changes, competitor promotions. After growth month: Identify what worked (campaign, channel, product) and scale it. Target >5-10% MoM for healthy growth.

07 Top SKUs by Net Revenue Horizontal Bar ▼

Ranks the top 10 SKUs by net revenue (gross revenue minus refunds). Horizontal bars make SKU names readable.

⚙ How It Works
Each SKU's net revenue is calculated and sorted in descending order. Top 10 displayed as horizontal bars to quickly identify best-selling products.
𝐀 Formula
Net Rev = SUM(gross_revenue) - SUM(refund_amount) GROUP BY master_sku

  • master_sku — SKU identifier

💡 What to Look For
Top SKU much higher than rest = High dependency risk. If that SKU has issues, significant revenue is at stake. Look at the Pareto count to understand concentration.
⚡ Actionable Insights
Ensure top SKUs are always in stock. Consider bundle deals pairing top SKUs with slower movers. If top SKU has high refund rate, investigate quality issues urgently.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Top-level stat cards on the Sales Analytics dashboard. These are your most important business health metrics.

K1 Gross Revenue KPI ▼

Total invoice amount before any deductions — the top-line sales figure.

⚙ How It Works
Aggregates the sum of all invoice amounts in the selected date range, before subtracting refunds, discounts, or taxes.
𝐀 Formula
Gross Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) WHERE status = 'Shipped'
📊 Data Used
  • invoice_amount — Total amount
  • order_status — Must be shipped
📈 Benchmark
Compare against previous period and same period last year. A healthy business shows consistent growth.
⚡ Actionable Insights
Use this as your north star metric. If declining, check: order volume drop (demand issue), AOV drop (pricing/mix issue), or refund spike (quality issue).
Monthly Revenue ChartNet RevenueAOV
K2 Net Revenue KPI ▼

Revenue retained after deducting refunds from gross revenue. The true revenue figure.

⚙ How It Works
Refund amounts are subtracted from gross revenue to show actual money retained from sales.
𝐀 Formula
Net Revenue = Gross Revenue - Total Refund Amount
📊 Data Used
  • gross_revenue — Total invoiced amount
  • refund_amount — Amount refunded
📈 Benchmark
Should be 85-95% of gross revenue depending on industry. If Net/Gross ratio drops below 80%, investigate refund causes urgently.
Gross RevenueRefund LossRev Leakage
K3 Refund Loss KPI ▼

Total amount refunded to customers in the selected period.

⚙ How It Works
Aggregates all refund transaction amounts. Represents total revenue returned to customers.
𝐀 Formula
Refund Loss = SUM(refund_amount) WHERE transaction_type = 'Refund'
📊 Data Used
  • refund_amount — Amount refunded
⚠ Red Flag
Any sudden increase in refund loss is a red flag. Investigate immediately: check for product defects, delivery damage, listing accuracy, or fraudulent returns.
Gross RevenueRefund RateNet Revenue
K4 Shipped Orders KPI ▼

Number of orders successfully shipped to customers.

⚙ How It Works
Counts distinct orders where the status is marked as shipped within the selected period.
𝐀 Formula
Shipped Orders = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) WHERE status = 'Shipped'
📊 Data Used
  • order_id — Unique order identifier
  • status — Order status
Total UnitsAOV
K5 Total Units KPI ▼

Total quantity of individual units sold across all shipped orders.

⚙ How It Works
Sums up the quantity field for all shipped order lines.
𝐀 Formula
Total Units = SUM(quantity) WHERE order_status = 'Shipped'
📊 Data Used
  • quantity — Units per line item
Shipped OrdersAOV
K6 Average Order Value (AOV) KPI ▼

Average revenue generated per shipped order.

⚙ How It Works
Gross revenue is divided by the number of shipped orders.
𝐀 Formula
AOV = Gross Revenue / Shipped Orders
📊 Data Used
  • gross_revenue — Total invoiced amount
  • shipped_orders — Count of shipped orders
📈 Benchmark
Higher AOV indicates customers are buying more per order. Track AOV trends alongside Gross Revenue to understand if growth comes from more orders or bigger orders.
⚡ Actionable Insights
To increase AOV: introduce product bundles, volume discounts, free shipping thresholds, or upsells at checkout.
Gross RevenueShipped Orders
K7 Refund Rate KPI ▼

Percentage of shipped orders that resulted in a refund.

⚙ How It Works
Refunded orders are divided by total shipped orders and converted to a percentage.
𝐀 Formula
Refund Rate = (Refunded Orders / Shipped Orders) × 100
📊 Data Used
  • refunded_orders — Orders with refunds
  • shipped_orders — Orders shipped
📈 Benchmark
Target <5% refund rate. Above 10% requires urgent attention. Above 20% indicates critical product or logistics issues.
⚠ Red Flag
If refund rate doubles compared to previous period, investigate: product quality, packaging damage, listing accuracy, or delivery partner issues.

Orders without a warehouse identifier.
