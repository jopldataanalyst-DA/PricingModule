1. Monthly Revenue Bar Chart

Displays the total gross revenue generated each month over the selected date range.

How It Works
The chart aggregates transaction data by calendar month. Each bar represents the sum of all invoice amounts (gross revenue) for that month. Data is sourced from sales transactions after applying filters (portal, account, fulfillment mode, etc.).
Formula
Monthly Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) GROUP BY MONTH(transaction_date)
Columns / Data Used
  • transaction_date — Date of the sale
  • invoice_amount — Gross amount of the invoice
  • portal — B2B / B2C (filtered)
What It Explains
Shows revenue trends over time — whether sales are growing, declining, or seasonal. Helps identify peak months and unexpected dips.
2. Fulfillment Channel Revenue Doughnut Chart

Shows the proportion of total revenue contributed by each fulfillment channel.

How It Works
Revenue is grouped by the fulfillment mode (e.g., FBA, FBM, Easy Ship). Each slice represents the percentage share of total revenue for that channel. The doughnut chart makes it easy to compare relative contributions.
Formula
Channel Revenue Share = (SUM(revenue) for channel / SUM(revenue) all channels) × 100
Columns / Data Used
  • fulfillment_channel — FBA / FBM / Easy Ship / etc.
  • invoice_amount — Revenue per transaction
What It Explains
Identifies which fulfillment channels drive the most revenue. Helps decide where to focus operational improvements.
3. Top States by Orders Bar Chart

Ranks states by the number of shipped orders placed from them.

How It Works
Orders are counted per billing/shipping state and sorted descending. The top 10 states are shown as bars for quick comparison.
Formula
Orders by State = COUNT(order_id) GROUP BY billing_state
Columns / Data Used
  • billing_state — Customer state
  • order_id — Unique order identifier
What It Explains
Highlights geographic demand concentration. Useful for targeting marketing, warehousing, and logistics planning.
4. Monthly Revenue Trend Line Chart (Area)

A filled line chart tracking gross revenue month-over-month across the entire period.

How It Works
Each point is the total gross revenue for a month. Points are connected by a smoothed line with a translucent fill below it, emphasizing the trend direction and magnitude.
Formula
Monthly Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) GROUP BY DATE_TRUNC('month', transaction_date)
Columns / Data Used
  • transaction_date — Date of transaction
  • invoice_amount — Gross invoice amount
What It Explains
Provides a clear visual of revenue trajectory — upward trends indicate growth, plateaus suggest stagnation, and declines signal issues needing attention.
5. Month-over-Month Growth Bar Chart (Colored)

Shows the percentage change in revenue from one month to the next, colored green for growth and red for decline.

How It Works
For each month after the first, the percentage difference from the previous month is calculated. Green bars indicate positive growth; red bars indicate negative growth (decline). The visual instantly signals good and bad months.
Formula
MoM Growth % = ((Revenue(current_month) - Revenue(previous_month)) / Revenue(previous_month)) × 100
Columns / Data Used
  • month — Month label
  • revenue — Aggregated monthly revenue
What It Explains
Reveals the momentum of the business. Consistent green bars indicate healthy growth; red bars require investigation into the cause of decline.
6. Channel Revenue Bar Chart

Compares gross revenue across different fulfillment/sales channels.

How It Works
Revenue is aggregated by channel. Each bar represents a channel's total revenue, making it easy to compare which channels perform best.
Formula
Channel Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) GROUP BY fulfillment_channel
Columns / Data Used
  • fulfillment_channel — Channel name
  • invoice_amount — Revenue amount
What It Explains
Identifies the highest and lowest performing channels for strategic decision-making.
7. Top SKUs by Net Revenue Horizontal Bar Chart

Ranks the top 10 SKUs by net revenue (gross revenue minus refunds).

How It Works
Each SKU's net revenue is calculated and sorted in descending order. The top 10 are displayed as horizontal bars so SKU names are readable. Helps quickly identify best-selling products.
Formula
Net Revenue = SUM(gross_revenue) - SUM(refund_amount) GROUP BY master_sku
Columns / Data Used
  • master_sku — SKU identifier
  • gross_revenue — Total revenue before refunds
  • refund_amount — Amount refunded
  • net_revenue — Revenue after refunds
What It Explains
Focuses inventory and marketing efforts on the most profitable products. Also indicates which SKUs contribute most to the bottom line.
8. Top ASINs by Revenue Horizontal Bar Chart

Shows the top ASINs by gross revenue.

How It Works
ASINs are grouped and their revenue summed. The highest revenue ASINs are shown as horizontal bars for easy scanning.
Formula
ASIN Revenue = SUM(revenue) GROUP BY Asin
Columns / Data Used
  • Asin — Amazon Standard Identification Number
  • revenue — Revenue attributed to the ASIN
What It Explains
Helps understand which Amazon listings generate the most revenue, useful for ad spend allocation and listing optimization.
9. Top 10 States by Net Revenue Bar Chart

Ranks the top 10 states by net revenue (after refunds).

How It Works
State-wise net revenue is calculated by subtracting refunds from gross revenue. States are sorted and the top 10 displayed as bars.
Formula
Net Revenue by State = SUM(gross_revenue - refund_amount) GROUP BY state
Columns / Data Used
  • billing_state — Customer state
  • gross_revenue — Revenue before refunds
  • refund_amount — Refunds issued
What It Explains
Reveals the most profitable states after accounting for refunds, guiding regional strategy.
10. Top Cities by Orders Bar Chart

Shows the cities with the highest number of shipped orders.

How It Works
Orders are counted per city (with state label) and sorted descending. The top 10 cities are displayed.
Formula
Orders by City = COUNT(order_id) GROUP BY billing_city, billing_state
Columns / Data Used
  • billing_city — City name
  • billing_state — State for disambiguation
  • order_id — Unique order
What It Explains
Highlights micro-level demand hubs for localized promotions and logistics planning.
11. Top Postal Codes by Revenue Bar Chart

Displays the postal codes that generate the highest revenue.

How It Works
Revenue is aggregated by pincode/postal code and sorted descending.
Formula
Revenue by Pincode = SUM(revenue) GROUP BY pincode
Columns / Data Used
  • pincode — Postal code
  • revenue — Total revenue
What It Explains
Pinpoints high-value postal codes for targeted logistics and marketing.
12. Origin States by Shipments Bar Chart

Shows which origin states ship the most orders.

How It Works
Shipment counts are aggregated by the origin warehouse state and sorted descending.
Formula
Shipments by Origin = COUNT(shipment_id) GROUP BY origin_state
Columns / Data Used
  • origin_state — Warehouse/fulfillment state
  • shipment_id — Shipment identifier
What It Explains
Identifies primary shipping hubs and potential warehouse capacity issues.
13. Top Routes by Volume Bar Chart

Shows the most frequently used origin→destination shipping routes.

How It Works
Each unique route (from_state → to_state) is counted by shipment volume. The top routes are displayed.
Formula
Route Volume = COUNT(shipment_id) GROUP BY origin_state, destination_state
Columns / Data Used
  • origin_state — Shipping origin
  • destination_state — Shipping destination
  • route_volume — Number of shipments on route
What It Explains
Highlights high-traffic shipping corridors for carrier contract negotiation and route optimization.
14. Warehouse Performance Dual-Axis Bar Chart

Plots both order count and revenue per warehouse on a single chart with two Y-axes.

How It Works
For each warehouse, two metrics are plotted: order count (left axis) and revenue in ₹K (right axis). This allows comparing both volume and value simultaneously.
Formula
Orders = COUNT(order_id) GROUP BY warehouse_id
Revenue = SUM(gross_revenue) GROUP BY warehouse_id
Columns / Data Used
  • warehouse — Warehouse name
  • orders — Order count
  • revenue — Total revenue
What It Explains
Identifies top-performing warehouses by both throughput and revenue, and highlights underperformers.
15. Fulfillment Channel Net Revenue Bar Chart

Net revenue (gross minus refunds) broken down by fulfillment channel.

How It Works
For each fulfillment channel, net revenue is calculated and displayed as a bar. Shows which channels contribute most profitably.
Formula
Net Revenue by Channel = SUM(gross_revenue - refund_amount) GROUP BY fulfillment_channel
Columns / Data Used
  • channel — Fulfillment channel name
  • net_revenue — Revenue after refunds
What It Explains
Highlights the most profitable channels when returns are factored in.
16. Fulfillment Orders Bar Chart

Number of orders per fulfillment channel.

How It Works
Orders are counted per fulfillment channel and displayed as bars.
Formula
Orders by Channel = COUNT(order_id) GROUP BY fulfillment_channel
Columns / Data Used
  • channel — Fulfillment channel name
  • orders — Order count
What It Explains
Reveals order volume distribution across channels — which channels handle the most orders.
17. Tax by State Bar Chart

Total tax collected broken down by state.

How It Works
Tax amounts (IGST + CGST + SGST) are aggregated per state and shown as bars.
Formula
Tax by State = SUM(igst + cgst + sgst) GROUP BY state
Columns / Data Used
  • state — State name
  • tax — Total tax amount collected
What It Explains
Helps understand tax liability distribution across states for compliance and reporting.
18. Tax by HSN Code Bar Chart

Tax amounts grouped by HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) code.

How It Works
Tax is aggregated per HSN code and displayed as bars. Shows which product categories contribute the most tax.
Formula
Tax by HSN = SUM(tax_amount) GROUP BY hsn_code
Columns / Data Used
  • hsn — HSN code
  • tax — Total tax amount
What It Explains
Useful for tax planning and understanding which product categories drive tax liability.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The top-level stat cards on the Sales Analytics dashboard showing high-level business metrics.

Gross Revenue KPI

Total invoice amount before any deductions — the top-line sales figure.

How It Works
Aggregates the sum of all invoice amounts in the selected date range, before subtracting refunds, discounts, or taxes.
Formula
Gross Revenue = SUM(invoice_amount) WHERE status = 'Shipped'
Columns / Data Used
  • invoice_amount — Total amount on the invoice
  • order_status — Must be shipped
What It Explains
Measures the overall sales volume. A key input for net revenue and refund rate calculations.
Net Revenue KPI

Revenue retained after deducting refunds from gross revenue.

How It Works
Refund amounts are subtracted from gross revenue to show the actual money retained from sales.
Formula
Net Revenue = Gross Revenue - Total Refund Amount
Columns / Data Used
  • gross_revenue — Total invoiced amount
  • refund_amount — Amount refunded
What It Explains
The true revenue figure after returns. Used for profitability analysis.
Refund Loss KPI

Total amount refunded to customers in the selected period.

How It Works
Aggregates all refund transaction amounts. Represents the total revenue that was returned to customers.
Formula
Refund Loss = SUM(refund_amount) WHERE transaction_type = 'Refund'
Columns / Data Used
  • refund_amount — Amount refunded per transaction
What It Explains
Quantifies revenue leakage. High refund loss indicates product quality, fulfillment, or customer satisfaction issues.
Shipped Orders KPI

Number of orders successfully shipped to customers.

How It Works
Counts distinct orders where the status is marked as shipped within the selected period.
Formula
Shipped Orders = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) WHERE status = 'Shipped'
Columns / Data Used
  • order_id — Unique order identifier
  • status — Order status
What It Explains
Indicates sales volume in terms of fulfilled orders. Used to compute AOV and shipment rate.
Total Units KPI

Total quantity of individual units sold across all shipped orders.

How It Works
Sums up the quantity field for all shipped order lines.
Formula
Total Units = SUM(quantity) WHERE order_status = 'Shipped'
Columns / Data Used
  • quantity — Units per line item
What It Explains
Measures true product volume independent of order count. Helps compute average units per order.
Average Order Value (AOV) KPI

Average revenue generated per shipped order.

How It Works
Gross revenue is divided by the number of shipped orders.
Formula
AOV = Gross Revenue / Shipped Orders
Columns / Data Used
  • gross_revenue — Total invoiced amount
  • shipped_orders — Count of shipped orders
What It Explains
Higher AOV indicates customers are buying more per order. Useful for pricing and bundling strategies.
Refund Rate KPI

Percentage of shipped orders that resulted in a refund.

How It Works
Refunded orders are divided by total shipped orders and converted to a percentage.
Formula
Refund Rate = (Refunded Orders / Shipped Orders) × 100
Columns / Data Used
  • refunded_orders — Orders with refunds
  • shipped_orders — Orders shipped
What It Explains
A critical quality metric. High refund rates indicate product, delivery, or customer satisfaction issues requiring attention.
Total Tax KPI

Total tax amount collected across all transactions.

How It Works
Aggregates all tax components (IGST + CGST + SGST) from all shipped transactions.
Formula
Total Tax = SUM(igst_amount + cgst_amount + sgst_amount)
Columns / Data Used
  • igst_amount — Integrated GST
  • cgst_amount — Central GST
  • sgst_amount — State GST
What It Explains
Shows the total tax liability. Useful for GST compliance and cash flow planning.

Transaction Quality & Health Metrics

Mini-stats in the Transactions tab showing order health and data quality indicators.

Total Orders Metric

Total count of all orders placed including shipped, refunded, and cancelled.

How It Works
Counts every unique order ID in the system for the selected filters.
Formula
Total Orders = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)
What It Explains
Baseline metric that all other transaction rates are calculated from.
Shipped Metric

Orders successfully fulfilled and shipped.

Formula
Shipped = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) WHERE status = 'Shipped'
What It Explains
Reflects fulfillment efficiency. Combined with Total Orders gives the shipment rate.
Refunded Metric

Orders where a refund was issued after shipment.

Formula
Refunded = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) WHERE refund_amount > 0
What It Explains
Direct measure of returns. High value suggests product or delivery issues.
Cancelled Metric

Orders cancelled before shipment.

Formula
Cancelled = COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) WHERE status = 'Cancelled'
What It Explains
High cancellation rates may indicate pricing issues, stock unavailability, or customer hesitation.
Net Shipment Rate Metric

Percentage of orders successfully shipped.

Formula
Net Shipment Rate = (Shipped / Total Orders) × 100
What It Explains
Overall fulfillment effectiveness. A low rate indicates many orders are being cancelled or lost.
Refund Rate (Transactions) Metric

Percentage of shipped orders refunded.

Formula
Refund Rate = (Refunded / Shipped) × 100
What It Explains
Same as the KPI refund rate but from the transaction perspective. Helps isolate transaction-level issues.
Cancel Rate Metric

Percentage of total orders cancelled.

Formula
Cancel Rate = (Cancelled / Total Orders) × 100
What It Explains
Helps measure the effectiveness of the order-to-fulfillment process.
Same-Day Refunds Anomaly

Orders refunded on the same day they were placed.

How It Works
Compares order date with refund date. Any refund occurring on the same date as the order is flagged.
What It Explains
May indicate fraudulent activity, system errors, or abuse of return policies. Worth investigating.
Return Loops Anomaly

Orders that went through multiple return/refund cycles.

How It Works
Identifies orders with more than one refund transaction associated with them.
What It Explains
Could indicate customer abuse, product quality issues, or systemic processing errors.
Multi-Refund Orders Anomaly

Orders where the refund was processed more than once.

What It Explains
Potential double-refund scenario that needs investigation to prevent revenue leakage.
Duplicate Invoices Data Quality

Count of invoice numbers that appear multiple times.

How It Works
Groups rows by invoice number and counts those with more than one occurrence.
Formula
Duplicate Invoices = COUNT(invoice_number) HAVING COUNT > 1
What It Explains
Data integrity issue. Duplicate invoice numbers can cause accounting and tax filing problems.

Product & SKU Metrics

Pareto SKUs Metric

Number of SKUs that contribute to 80% of total net revenue.

How It Works
SKUs are sorted by net revenue descending. The smallest set of SKUs whose cumulative revenue reaches 80% of total is counted.
Formula
Pareto Count = MIN(n) WHERE SUM(top_n_SKUs_revenue) >= 0.8 × Total_Net_Revenue
What It Explains
A low Pareto count means a few SKUs drive most revenue (80/20 rule). Helps focus inventory and marketing on top performers.
Total Net Revenue Metric

Sum of net revenue across all SKUs.

Formula
Total Net Revenue = SUM(gross_revenue - refund_amount) GROUP BY SKU
What It Explains
Same as the KPI net revenue but shown within the Products tab for context.

Geography Metrics

Interstate % Metric

Percentage of shipments going to different states (cross-border).

Formula
Interstate % = (Interstate Shipments / Total Shipments) × 100
What It Explains
High interstate % means most sales cross state lines. Subject to IGST and longer shipping times.
Intrastate % Metric

Percentage of shipments within the same state.

Formula
Intrastate % = (Intrastate Shipments / Total Shipments) × 100
What It Explains
Higher intrastate % indicates strong local demand. Subject to CGST + SGST.

Tax & Compliance Metrics

Total Tax Metric

Sum of all tax collected across transactions.

Formula
Total Tax = SUM(igst + cgst + sgst)
Effective Tax Rate Metric

Overall tax rate as a percentage of gross revenue.

Formula
Effective Tax Rate = (Total Tax / Gross Revenue) × 100
What It Explains
Should align with expected GST rates. Deviations may indicate tax filing or classification issues.
IGST (Interstate) Metric

Integrated GST collected on cross-state transactions.

What It Explains
Tax component for interstate sales. Higher IGST relative to CGST+SGST indicates more cross-state business.
CGST + SGST (Intrastate) Metric

Central GST + State GST collected on same-state transactions.

What It Explains
Tax component for intrastate sales. Higher values indicate strong local market presence.
Missing HSN Rows Data Quality

Transaction rows where HSN code is missing.

What It Explains
Data quality issue for GST compliance. HSN codes are mandatory for tax filing. Should be resolved promptly.

Data Quality Metrics

Avg Qty/Order Metric

Average number of units per order.

Formula
Avg Qty/Order = Total Units / Total Orders
What It Explains
Indicates bulk buying behavior. Higher values may suggest wholesale or B2B purchases.
Missing Invoice # Data Quality

Orders where the invoice number field is blank.

What It Explains
Critical data quality issue. Missing invoice numbers can block GST filing and reconciliation.
Missing Warehouse ID Data Quality

Orders without a warehouse identifier.

What It Explains
Operational data quality issue. Hinders warehouse performance tracking and logistics analysis.
Zero-Amt Shipments Data Quality

Shipments recorded with zero invoice amount.

What It Explains
Likely data entry errors. These should be reviewed and corrected to ensure accurate revenue reporting.
Negative Amt (Non-Refund) Data Quality

Transactions with negative amounts that are not refunds.

What It Explains
Data anomalies. Negative non-refund amounts may indicate system errors or data corruption.
Orphan Refunds Data Quality

Refund records that cannot be matched to any original order.

What It Explains
Data integrity issue. Orphan refunds mean money was refunded without a corresponding sale, which is a compliance risk.
Duplicate Invoice Groups Data Quality

Groups of transactions sharing the same invoice number.

What It Explains
Duplicate invoices can cause incorrect revenue aggregation and GST filing errors. Should be investigated.

Executive-level view of money lost to refunds. Critical for profitability assessment.
Executive Refund Rate Metric

Net Shipment Rate Metric

Percentage of orders successfully shipped without refund. Key operational health metric.

Effective Tax Rate Metric

Overall effective tax rate shown in Executive tab.

Top SKU Insight

The single highest revenue-generating SKU.

What It Explains
Identifies the most important product in the portfolio. Helps prioritize inventory and marketing.
Top State Insight

The state generating the highest revenue.

What It Explains
Identifies the most valuable geographic market. Useful for regional strategy and warehousing decisions.

Tables & Data Grids

Detailed tabular views across all dashboard tabs.

GSTIN Revenue Table Table

Revenue and orders broken down by GSTIN (GST Identification Number).

Columns
  • GSTIN — GST Identification Number of the customer
  • Revenue — Total revenue from that GSTIN
  • Orders — Number of orders from that GSTIN
What It Explains
Helps track B2B vs B2C performance. B2B customers typically have a GSTIN and higher order values.
Duplicate Invoices Table Table

Invoice numbers that appear more than once and their occurrence count.

Columns
  • Invoice Number — The duplicated invoice
  • Count — How many times it appears
What It Explains
Helps identify and fix data entry or system issues causing invoice duplication.
Cancel-to-Ship Ratio Table Table

Comparison of cancelled vs shipped orders per GSTIN with cancel rate.

Columns
  • GSTIN — GST Identification Number
  • Cancels — Cancelled order count
  • Ships — Shipped order count
  • Cancel Rate — Cancel / (Cancel + Ship) × 100
SKU Performance Table Table

Detailed performance metrics for each SKU.

Columns
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit
  • Gross Rev — Gross revenue before refunds
  • Refund — Refund amount
  • Net Rev — Revenue after refunds
  • Units — Quantity sold
  • Refund Rate — Refund / Gross × 100
  • Tax Rate — Tax / Gross × 100
Zero-Refund & High-Refund Risk Table

Two tables: SKUs with no refunds (restock candidates) and SKUs with high refund rates (quality concerns).

Columns (Zero-Refund)
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit
  • Net Rev — Net revenue
  • Units — Quantity sold
Columns (High-Refund)
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit
  • Refund Rate — Percentage refunded
  • Units — Quantity sold
Top HSN Codes Table Table

HSN codes ranked by taxable revenue.

Columns
  • HSN — Harmonized System Nomenclature code
  • Taxable Revenue — Revenue subject to tax
State-wise Performance Table Table

Orders, revenue, refunds, and net revenue broken down by state.

Columns
  • State — State name
  • Orders — Order count
  • Revenue — Gross revenue
  • Refund — Refund amount
  • Net Revenue — Revenue after refunds
  • Refund Rate — Refund / Revenue × 100
Routes & High-Refund Routes Tables Table

Shipping routes by volume/revenue and routes with high refund counts.

Columns (Routes)
  • From — Origin state
  • To — Destination state
  • Volume — Number of shipments
  • Revenue — Total revenue
Columns (Refund Routes)
  • From — Origin state
  • To — Destination state
  • Refunds — Refund count
Warehouse & Fulfillment Tables Table

Warehouse performance metrics and fulfillment channel breakdown.

Columns (Warehouse)
  • Warehouse — Warehouse name
  • Orders — Order count
  • Revenue — Total revenue
  • Refund Orders — Refunded order count
  • Refund Rate — Refund / Orders × 100
  • Rev/Order — Revenue per order
Columns (Fulfillment)
  • Channel — Fulfillment channel
  • Orders — Order count
  • Gross Rev — Gross revenue
  • Net Rev — Net revenue
  • Units — Quantity
  • Refund Rate — Refund percentage
  • AOV — Average order value
Operational Intelligence Section

High-level operational insights including restock candidates and stockout signals.

Restock Candidates Table Table

SKUs with high sales velocity and low refund rates — ideal for restocking.

Columns
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit
  • Units — Quantity sold
  • Net Revenue — Revenue after refunds
  • Refund Rate — Low refund percentage
What It Explains
Prioritise these SKUs for inventory replenishment. They sell well and have minimal returns.
Stockout Signals Table Table

SKUs that may be going out of stock soon based on current velocity.

Columns
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit
  • Units — Quantity sold
  • Net Revenue — Revenue after refunds
What It Explains
Proactive warning to reorder before stock runs out, preventing lost sales.
Key Insights Section

Executive summary showing top SKU, top state, top channel, Pareto count, revenue leakage, and net shipment rate.

What It Explains
A bird's-eye view of business health. Combines the most important metrics in one place for quick decision-making.
